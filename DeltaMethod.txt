### Abstract

We present the **Harmonic Scientific Method (HSM)**, a complete, falsifiable framework for detecting, testing, and controlling low-order phase couplings in complex systems. Building on the **Low-Order Wins** renormalization-group principle, HSM operationalizes the hypothesis that persistent structure in coupled oscillatory systems concentrates at small integer frequency ratios $(p{:}q)$ with $p+q le P$. The method specifies: (i) four foundational axioms grounding all inferences in phase-space observables and admissible transformations; (ii) a **locking kernel** $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ that penalizes both relation order and frequency detuning, yielding dimensionless coupling scores; (iii) **Edge tests E1‚ÄìE4** serving as hard falsification gates (vibration proof, symmetry invariance, micro-perturbation causality, out-of-sample persistence); (iv) a **Delta Coupling Operator (DCO)** that maps observed phases to accepted locks, primary graphs, RG-consistency flags, harmony gains, and minimal steering plans; (v) pre-registered null families (spectral, phase-shuffle, mechanistic surrogates) with BH-FDR control at $q le 0.05$; and (vi) two compact theorems proving RG irrelevance of high-order/detuned couplings and monotone harmony improvement under greedy low-order steering.

The framework is **platform-agnostic**: no code dependencies, only abstract operators and falsifiable predictions. We prove that under coarse-graining by scale $ell$, couplings obey $\frac{dK}{d\ell} = (2 - \Delta_{p:q})K - \Lambda K^3$ with $Delta_{p:q} = d + eta(p+q) + zeta|omega_b/omega_a - p/q|$, forcing decay of high-order modes. Acceptance requires clearing all null thresholds **and** passing all four Edge tests‚Äîno partial credit. Version 1.1 extends to non-sinusoidal clocks (event trains, semantic templates, mixed-radix cycles), adds observer-swap persistence to E2/E4, enforces micro-nudge monotonicity in E3, and introduces lock-churn $\chi$ to characterize adaptive regimes.

We provide a one-command reproducibility capsule, explicit failure modes (nonstationarity, aliasing, octave confusion), quantitative predictions for neural PAC, Josephson arrays, optomechanics, and biological rhythms, and a complete researcher-degrees-of-freedom ledger. The method elevates findings from "statistical fit" to **law-status** via persistence under symmetry + RG + cross-domain integer stability, offering a rigorous alternative to post-hoc pattern-finding in complex time series.

# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Motivation: The Problem of Spurious Structure

Across neuroscience, nonlinear dynamics, quantum systems, and biological networks, researchers routinely report "cross-frequency coupling," "mode-locking," "phase synchronization," and "harmonic relationships." Yet a troubling pattern persists: **most reported couplings fail independent replication**, exhibit parameter sensitivity consistent with overfitting, and lack principled rejection criteria.

The core pathology is threefold:

**1. Post-hoc flexibility.** Standard workflows permit dozens of undocumented choices (filter orders, window lengths, coupling estimators, significance thresholds), enabling researchers to "search until significant" without controlling family-wise error.

**2. Absence of hard falsification gates.** Conventional methods test whether coupling $K \neq 0$ against a single null (often inadequate phase-scrambling) but impose no mechanistic consistency checks. A coupling can "pass" statistical tests yet violate basic physical constraints (e.g., fail time-reversal symmetry, disappear under coarse-graining, exhibit non-integer frequency ratios).

**3. No operational connection to dynamical principles.** Coupling estimates are treated as final outputs rather than inputs to a **testable generative model**. If low-order integer ratios truly dominate‚Äîas suggested by Arnold tongues, KAM theory, and the empirical ubiquity of simple harmonic relationships‚Äîthis should manifest as **falsifiable structure** in how couplings decay with order, persist under perturbation, and respond to controlled interventions.

The **Harmonic Scientific Method** addresses these failures by **inverting the workflow**: instead of searching for couplings and post-hoc testing their significance, we:

- **Pre-register** a complete inference pipeline (axioms, estimators, nulls, gates, control protocol);
- **Derive** testable predictions from the Low-Order Wins RG principle;
- **Enforce** four hard Edge tests (E1‚ÄìE4) as logical prerequisites for any downstream claim;
- **Report** a single composite score (DeltaScore) aggregating lock strength, harmony gain, RG persistence, and out-of-sample validation;
- **Falsify cleanly** when any gate fails, with explicit enumeration of alternative mechanisms.

## 1.2 Core Principle: Low-Order Wins as Operational Law

The foundation is the **Low-Order Wins (LOW)** principle[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Low-Order-Wins-A-Renormalization-Group-Principle-for-Integer-Relation-Persistence-f5577d610f8c4470a8160254a620751c?pvs=21), which states that after detuning normalization and fixed coarse-graining, effective scaling dimensions $\Delta_{(p,q)}$ increase with relation order $r = p + q$. Under the RG flow

$$
\frac{dK_{(p,q)}}{d\ell} = \bigl(2 - \Delta_{(p,q)} - \Lambda K_{(p,q)}\bigr) K_{(p,q)},
$$

couplings with $\Delta_{(p,q)} < 2$ persist (RG-relevant) while those with $\Delta_{(p,q)} > 2$ decay exponentially (RG-irrelevant). Since $\Delta_{(p,q)} \approx d + \eta(p+q) + \zeta|\text{detune}|$ increases with both order and detuning, **only low-order, near-tuned ratios survive** repeated coarse-graining.

HSM operationalizes LOW by:

- Defining a **locking kernel** $K_{ab}(p{:}q) = \langle \sin(p\theta_b - q\theta_a) \rangle \cdot e^{-\lambda(p+q)} \cdot e^{-\gamma|\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q|}$ that explicitly penalizes order and detune;
- Requiring **Edge test E4** to verify high-order decay under coarse-graining;
- Using **micro-perturbations (E3)** to confirm that nudging frequencies toward hypothesized locks increases $K$ and harmony $H^*$;
- Constructing **null families** that preserve spectral structure while destroying cross-phase information, then demanding that observed locks beat **all three null types** simultaneously.

# 2. Foundational Axioms and Notation

## 2.1 Objects, Notation, and Units

### Phase Processes

Each channel $a \in A$ is observed as a **phase process** $(\theta_a(t), \rho_a(t), \omega_a(t))$ where:

- $\theta_a(t) \in \mathbb{R}$ is the **unwrapped phase** (radians);
- $\rho_a(t) \ge 0$ is the **amplitude** (dimensionless or native units);
- $\omega_a(t) = \dot{\theta}_a(t)$ is the **instantaneous frequency** (units: $mathrm{s}^{-1}$).

### Analysis Windows

For a population $A = {a_1, ldots, a_N}$, define expectations $\langle \cdot \rangle$ over a fixed analysis window $[t_0, t_0 + T]$ with duration $T$ and sampling rate $f_s$.

### Dimensionless Convention

**All coupling statistics are dimensionless.** Frequencies retain $\mathrm{s}^{-1}$ but enter coupling kernels only as **ratios** $omega_b/omega_a$.

### Octave Bands and Identifiability

**Octave band:** Frequency interval $[Omega, 2Omega)$.

**Identifiability constraint:** In each band, accept at most **one** primary ratio $(p{:}q)$ per ordered pair $(a,b)$. This prevents aliasing and ensures uniqueness.

### Integer Relations

A relation $(p,q)$ with $p, q \in \mathbb{N}, p+q \le P$ (preregistered order bound) corresponds to the resonance condition

$$
p \omega_b \approx q \omega_a.
$$

We call $r := p + q$ the **order** of the relation.

## 2.2 Clock Compiler (Extended for v1.1)

### Purpose

Not all systems admit sinusoidal oscillations. HSM must handle:

- **Event trains** (spikes, zero-crossings, renewal processes);
- **Symbolic streams** (ritual cadences, narrative beats, protocol steps);
- **Mixed-radix cycles** (calendar systems, musical pitch classes).

The **clock compiler** maps any source $x(t)$ to an admissible phase $theta_x(t)$.

### Compilation Rules

**1. Oscillatory signals** (default)

- Apply Hilbert transform: $x_{text{analytic}}(t) = x(t) + imathcal{H}[x(t)]$;
- Extract instantaneous phase: $theta(t) = arg(x_{text{analytic}}(t))$;
- Unwrap to ensure continuity.

**2. Event trains**

- Identify events $\{t_1, t_2, \ldots, t_n\}$ (e.g., spike times);
- Construct **renewal clock**: $\theta(t) = 2\pi \frac{t - t_k}{t_{k+1} - t_k}$ for $t in [t_k, t_{k+1})$;
- Phase resets to 0 at each event.

**3. Symbolic/semantic streams**

- Choose a small basis $\{T_1, \ldots, T_m\}$ of recurring templates (e.g., weekly ritual, narrative arc);
- Define $\theta_{\text{tpl}}(t)$ as the phase in the template cycle;
- Example: for weekly cycle, $theta(t) = 2pi cdot (text{day-of-week}(t) / 7)$.

**4. Mixed-radix circular tasks**

- Pick modulus $N$ aligned to the phenomenon:
    - $N = 12$ for pitch classes (chromatic scale);
    - $N = 7$ for weekly cycles;
    - $N = 24$ for circadian hours.
- Map observable $x(t) \in \{0, 1, \ldots, N-1\}$ to $theta(t) = 2pi x(t)/N$.

### Admissibility Requirement

All derived $\theta$ **must pass E1 (vibration legibility)** and **E2 (symmetry invariance)** or they are inadmissible. This prevents arbitrary "phase" assignments that have no operational meaning.

## 2.3 The Four Axioms

### A1 (Vibration)

**Statement:** Every observable phenomenon admits a phase description $\mathcal{V}(x) = (A, f, \phi, \mathbf{H})$ where:

- $A$: amplitude;
- $f$: fundamental frequency;
- $phi$: phase;
- $mathbf{H}$: harmonic content.

**Interpretation:** The axiom asserts that oscillatory structure is **observable**, not metaphorical. If a putative "phase" $\theta$ cannot be measured independently of the coupling analysis, it violates A1.

**Operational test:** Phase extraction must survive **amplitude muting** (multiply signal by zero, recover $\theta$ from zero-crossings or prior model). Encoded in **E1**.

---

### A2 (Closure)

**Statement:** All valid inferences are built from $\mathcal{V}$ and **admissible transformations** (filtering, coarse-graining, symmetry actions) without importing extraneous structure.

**Interpretation:** You cannot "discover" structure you pre-assumed. Example violations:

- Using a wavelet basis tailored to a specific $(p,q)$ before testing whether that relation exists;
- Choosing filter cutoffs to maximize apparent coupling;
- Applying causal filters that inject artificial directionality.

**Operational test:** All preprocessing must be preregistered and **apply uniformly** to all $(p,q)$ candidates. Encoded in **E2** (symmetry invariance).

---

### A3 (Observer Discipline)

**Statement:** Observers must preserve phase meaning under admissible transformations; otherwise claims are void.

**Interpretation:** If changing measurement apparatus or platform destroys the claimed coupling, the coupling is **observer-specific** (artifact) rather than **system-intrinsic** (real).

**Operational test (v1.1 extension):** **E2** and **E4** now require **observer/instrument swap** persistence. A lock that survives temporal coarse-graining but disappears when switching from EEG to MEG (at comparable SNR) fails A3.

---

### A4 (Quality Identity)

**Statement:** Among candidate descriptions $Q$, choose

$$
Q^* = \arg\max_Q \Big[ I(Q; O) - \lambda \mathrm{MDL}(Q) \Big],
$$

where $O$ ranges over **admissible observers obeying A1‚ÄìA3** and $\lambda > 0$ is preregistered.

**Interpretation:** "Quality" is the **most informative description penalized by complexity**, computed over observers that don't smuggle structure. This formalizes Occam's razor: prefer the simplest model that explains the data **without** assuming the conclusion.

**Operational consequence:** All choices (lock acceptance, model class, controller design) inherit this MDL discipline. The **DeltaScore** (Sec. 7) includes an MDL penalty term $-alpha_5 mathrm{MDL}$.

<aside>
üí°

**Why these axioms?** They rule out the most common failure modes in coupling analysis:

- A1: vacuous "phase" definitions;
- A2: circular reasoning (assuming structure then "finding" it);
- A3: platform artifacts mistaken for real effects;
- A4: overfitting (complex models that don't generalize).
</aside>

# 3. Coherence, Harmony, and the Locking Kernel

## 3.1 Pairwise Phase Coherence

For channels $a, b$, define

$$
C_{ab} = \Big| \big\langle e^{i(\theta_a - \theta_b)} \big\rangle \Big| \in [0, 1].
$$

This is the **magnitude of the mean phase difference**, a standard measure of phase synchronization.

---

## 3.2 Population Order Parameter

For a population $A = {a_1, ldots, a_N}$, the **Kuramoto order parameter** is

$$
R = \Big| \big\langle e^{i\theta} \big\rangle \Big| \in [0, 1],
$$

where $\langle \cdot \rangle$ averages over all channels.

**Interpretation:** $R \approx 1$ indicates strong global synchrony; $R \approx 0$ indicates incoherence.

**Acceptance requirement:** HSM requires $R > \texttt{accept\_R}$ (preregistered) for $\ge \texttt{min\_persist\_cycles}$ (preregistered) as a prerequisite for claiming any locks. This ensures the system exhibits **sustained coherence** before we search for specific $(p,q)$ relations.

---

## 3.3 Harmony Functional $H^*$

Define a **preregistered weighted sum**:

$$
H^* = w_C \overline{C} + w_S S + w_T T - w_D D + w_I I,
$$

where:

- $overline{C}$: mean pairwise coherence over **accepted edges** (not all pairs);
- $S$: **stability** = fraction of the analysis window where coherence exceeds threshold and persists $\ge$ fixed cycle count;
- $T$: **throughput** = domain-specific flow measure (e.g., effective bandwidth, task completion rate), normalized to $[0,1]$;
- $D$: **dissonance** = phase variance or detuning spread;
- $I$: **innovation** = emergence rate of **new** accepted locks per unit time.

All weights $w_\bullet$ and thresholds are **preregistered hyperparameters**.

**Interpretation:** $H^*$ quantifies system "harmony" as a balance of coherence, stability, productive flow, and creative flexibility. The **control objective** (Sec. 7, Theorem 2) is to increase $H^*$ via minimal frequency nudges.

### Lock-Churn (v1.1 Addition)

For adaptive systems, define

$$
\chi = \frac{\# \text{ primary lock switches}}{\text{analysis window}}.
$$

High $\chi$ indicates the system frequently switches dominant locks ("aperiodic creativity"); low $\chi$ indicates static locking. Report $\chi$ per pair/system to characterize regimes: lock $\to$ detune $\to$ re-lock cycles.

## 3.4 Locking Kernel $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ (v1.1 Updated)

For channels $a, b$ and coprime integers $p, q \ge 1$ with $p + q \le P$ (preregistered):

$$
\boxed{
K_{ab}(p{:}q) = \underbrace{\Big| \big\langle e^{i(p\theta_b - q\theta_a)} \big\rangle \Big|}_{\text{phase lock magnitude}} \cdot e^{-\lambda(p+q)} \cdot e^{-\gamma \delta_{ab}(p{:}q)}
}
$$

with **scale-free detuning**:

$$
\delta_{ab}(p{:}q) = \frac{\Big| \frac{\omega_b}{\omega_a} - \frac{p}{q} \Big|}{\max\!\Big( \frac{\omega_b}{\omega_a}, \frac{p}{q} \Big)}.
$$

### Components

**1. Phase lock magnitude** (v1.1 change)

Use the **circular magnitude** $\big| \langle e^{i(\cdot)} \rangle \big|$ rather than the signed projection $langle sin(cdot) rangle$. This makes $K \in [0,1]$ independent of phase origin.

**Directional evidence:** If causality testing is needed, keep the signed projection $\langle \sin(p\theta_b - q\theta_a) \rangle$ as a **separate diagnostic** (used in E3), but do **not** mix it into the acceptance kernel.

**2. Order penalty** $e^{-\lambda(p+q)}$

Exponentially suppresses high-order relations. Typical value: $\lambda \approx 0.6$ (inherited from LOW[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Low-Order-Wins-A-Renormalization-Group-Principle-for-Integer-Relation-Persistence-f5577d610f8c4470a8160254a620751c?pvs=21), but HSM treats this as a **preregistered hyperparameter**, not a universal constant).

**3. Detuning penalty** $e^{-\gamma \delta_{ab}(p{:}q)}$

Exponentially suppresses relations with large frequency mismatch. The scale-free form $\delta_{ab} = |\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q| / \max(\omega_b/\omega_a, p/q)$ ensures dimensionless comparison. Typical value: $gamma approx 12$.

### Units and Identifiability

- **Units:** $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ is dimensionless.
- **Identifiability:** Per $(a,b)$ and octave band (or mod-$N$ band for mixed-radix), accept at most **one** primary $(p{:}q)$. If multiple candidates exceed threshold, choose the one with **largest** $K$ after null-adjusted significance.

# 4. Null Families and Acceptance Criteria

## 4.1 Three Preregistered Null Families

To claim that an observed $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ represents **real structure** rather than noise or artifact, it must beat **all three** null types simultaneously:

### Null 1: Spectral Surrogates (Phase Randomization)

**Construction:**

1. Compute FFT of band-passed signal $x_a(t)$;
2. Randomize phases: $widetilde{X}*a(f) = |X_a(f)| e^{iphi*{text{rand}}(f)}$, $phi_{text{rand}} sim text{Uniform}[0, 2pi)$;
3. Inverse FFT to get $widetilde{x}_a(t)$;
4. Re-estimate $widetilde{K}_{ab}(p{:}q)$.

**What it destroys:** Cross-channel phase relationships.

**What it preserves:** Amplitude spectra, autocorrelation within each channel.

**Purpose:** Rules out spurious coupling from spectral overlap or waveform shape.

---

### Null 2: Phase-Shuffle Surrogates (Temporal Permutation)

**Construction:**

1. Extract phases $theta_a(t), theta_b(t)$;
2. Circularly shift one phase by random lag $tau sim text{Uniform}[0, T)$: $widetilde{theta}_b(t) = theta_b(t + tau)$;
3. Re-estimate $widetilde{K}_{ab}(p{:}q)$.

**What it destroys:** Temporal alignment of cross-channel phases.

**What it preserves:** Marginal phase distributions, within-channel dynamics.

**Purpose:** Rules out coupling from slow drifts or common external driver.

---

### Null 3: Mechanistic Surrogates (AR/ARMA)

**Construction:**

1. Fit AR or ARMA model to each channel's amplitude/frequency time series;
2. Simulate from the fitted model to generate synthetic data $widetilde{x}_a(t), widetilde{x}_b(t)$;
3. Re-estimate $widetilde{K}_{ab}(p{:}q)$.

**What it destroys:** Cross-channel coupling.

**What it preserves:** Within-channel autocovariance structure.

**Purpose:** Rules out coupling from autocorrelation patterns that "look like" locks.

---

## 4.2 Edge Condition (Statistical Rigor)

A candidate lock $(a,b,p,q)$ must satisfy:

$$
K_{ab}(p{:}q) > \max\!\big\{ \tau_{\text{spec}}^{95}, \tau_{\text{shuffle}}^{95}, \tau_{\text{mech}}^{95} \big\},
$$

where $\tau_{\text{type}}^{95}$ is the 95th percentile of the null distribution for that type (500 replicates).

**Multiple-test control:** Apply **Benjamini-Hochberg FDR** at $q \le 0.05$ across all $(p,q)$ candidates in the roster.

<aside>
üî¨

**Why three nulls?** Each null type targets a different alternative hypothesis:

- Spectral: waveform harmonics or filterbank leakage;
- Phase-shuffle: slow drift or common modulation;
- Mechanistic: autocorrelation mimicking coupling.

Beating **all three** provides much stronger evidence than a single null test.

</aside>

## 4.3 Full Acceptance Criteria (All Required)

A lock $(a,b,p,q)$ is **accepted** if and only if:

**1. Population coherence:** $R > \texttt{accept\_R}$ for $ge texttt{min_persist_cycles}$.

**2. Null threshold:** $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ exceeds all three null thresholds (Sec. 4.2).

**3. Edge tests E1‚ÄìE4 pass** (Sec. 6).

**Doctrine:** Failing **any** criterion **invalidates** the lock. There is **no partial credit**.

# 5. RG Flow and the Low-Order Wins Law

## 5.1 The Flow Equation

Under coarse-graining by scale $\ell$ (e.g., temporal decimation, spatial pooling), the coupling strength $K = K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ evolves as:

$$
\frac{dK}{d\ell} = \bigl( 2 - \Delta_{p:q} \bigr) K - \Lambda K^3 + \cdots,
$$

where the **effective scaling dimension** is

$$
\Delta_{p:q} = d + \eta(p+q) + \zeta \Big| \frac{\omega_b}{\omega_a} - \frac{p}{q} \Big|.
$$

### Parameters

- $d$: **base dimensionality** of the frequency space (typically $d = 2$ for two-oscillator systems; see Appendix A of LOW[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Low-Order-Wins-A-Renormalization-Group-Principle-for-Integer-Relation-Persistence-f5577d610f8c4470a8160254a620751c?pvs=21) for the geometric derivation);
- $eta > 0$: **order penalty coefficient** (preregistered or estimated);
- $zeta > 0$: **detuning penalty coefficient** (preregistered or estimated);
- $Lambda > 0$: **nonlinear damping** (bounds growth of strong couplings).

---

## 5.2 The Low-Order Wins Law (Operational Statement)

**Corollary (RG Irrelevance of High Order / Detune):**

If $p+q$ is large **or** detuning $|\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q|$ is large, then $\Delta_{p:q} > 2$ and:

$$
K^* = 0 \quad \text{(unique stable fixed point)}.
$$

Hence, **only low-order, near-tuned locks persist** under noise and coarse-graining.

**Proof Sketch:**

$\Delta_{p:q}$ increases monotonically in both $p+q$ and detune. For sufficiently large values, $2 - Delta_{p:q} < 0$, making the linear term negative. The cubic term $-\Lambda K^3$ stabilizes $K = 0$ as the unique attractor. See Appendix B for full proof.

- The detuning penalty $e^{-\gamma \delta}$ mirrors the $\zeta|\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q|$ term.

Thus, **empirical** $K$ **values should exhibit RG-consistent decay**‚Äîwhich is exactly what **Edge test E4** verifies.

# 6. Edge Tests: The Four Hard Gates

Every accepted lock **must pass all four Edge tests**. Failing **any** test **invalidates all downstream claims**. No partial credit.

---

## E1 ‚Äî Channelized Vibration Proof

### Test

**1. Amplitude muting:** Set amplitude $\rho_a(t) = 0$ (or inject high-amplitude noise). Phase $\theta_a(t)$ must remain recoverable from:

- Zero-crossings of the original signal;
- A previously fitted phase model;
- Independent measurement (e.g., separate sensor).

**2. Time-reversal:** Reverse time $t to -t$. Undirected measures (coherence $C_{ab}$, kernel magnitude $|K|$) must be preserved; directed measures (phase lag, Granger causality) must reverse sign.

### Decision Rule

If either check fails, **halt pipeline** and reject all locks for that channel. The "phase" is not operationally meaningful (violates **A1 Vibration**).

### Rationale

This test prevents vacuous "phase" definitions where $\theta$ is just a transformed version of the raw signal with no independent existence.

---

## E2 ‚Äî Symmetry & Observer Invariance (v1.1 Tightened)

### Test

**1. Phase gauge invariance:** Adding a constant $\theta_a \to \theta_a + \phi_0$ must not change $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ (up to numerical precision).

**2. Permutation invariance:** If channels are exchangeable (e.g., symmetric oscillators, identical sensors), permuting their labels must preserve the lock set.

**3. Observer/instrument swap (v1.1 addition):** Repeat measurement with a different platform or sensor modality (e.g., EEG $\to$ MEG; optical $\to$ electrical; high-rate $\to$ downsampled). Accepted locks must persist with **comparable** $K$ **values** (within bootstrapped 95% CI).

### Decision Rule

If any symmetry is broken or observer-swap fails, **invalidate** the lock. The claimed structure is either **preprocessing artifact** (violates **A2 Closure**) or **observer-specific** (violates **A3 Observer Discipline**).

### Rationale

Spurious structure often breaks symmetries that the physical system respects. Example: applying a causal filter (e.g., forward-only Butterworth) injects artificial directionality, breaking time-reversal symmetry.

The observer-swap requirement ensures that locks are **system-intrinsic**, not measurement-dependent.

---

## E3 ‚Äî Micro-Perturbation Causality (v1.1 Monotonicity Required)

### Test

**Setup:** Choose the top-scoring lock $(a^*, b^*, p^*, q^*)$ (highest $K$ after null adjustment). Compute the detuning:

$$
\delta\omega^* = p^* \omega_{b^*} - q^* \omega_{a^*}.
$$

**Intervention:** Apply small, **budgeted** frequency nudges $\{\delta\omega_a\}$ with $\sum_a |\delta\omega_a| \le \epsilon$ (preregistered budget, e.g., $epsilon = 0.01 cdot overline{omega}$) in the direction that **reduces** $|deltaomega^*|$.

**Prediction:** The perturbation should:

1. **Increase** $K_{a^* b^*}(p^*{:}q^*)$ (stronger lock);
2. **Increase** harmony $H^*$ (better system-wide coherence);
3. **Decrease** $|\delta\omega^*|$ (closer to exact resonance).

**Control:** Simultaneously test **placebo nudges** (random directions, same $\epsilon$ budget) and **null controllers** (no intervention). The aligned nudge must outperform both.

**v1.1 Monotonicity requirement:** The improvement must be **monotone**: if tiny aligned nudges ($epsilon/10$) produce **smaller** gains than expected by linear extrapolation, or if gains **reverse sign** at $epsilon/2$, **demote the claim** (likely spurious or unstable lock).

### Decision Rule

If the prediction fails for **any** of the three outcomes, or if monotonicity fails, **reject** the lock. The claimed $(p^*, q^*)$ does **not** reflect a real dynamical attractor (violates the RG flow's implied causal structure).

### Rationale

This is the **strongest** test. It moves beyond passive observation to **active manipulation**. If nudging the system toward a hypothesized lock **doesn't** strengthen it, the lock was either:

- A statistical fluctuation;
- Misidentified $(p,q)$;
- Genuine but **not causal** (e.g., both channels driven by a hidden third variable).

**E3 is what elevates HSM from "correlation detection" to "mechanistic validation."**

---

## E4 ‚Äî Out-of-Sample & Cross-Modality RG Persistence (v1.1 Extended)

### Test

**1. Temporal hold-out:** Split data into training (first 70%) and test (last 30%). Estimate $K_{ab}(p{:}q)$ on training, verify $K_{ab}^{\text{test}} \ge \tau'$ (slightly relaxed threshold to account for finite-sample variance).

**2. Coarse-graining:** Apply the RG operator (temporal pooling, spectral binning, downsampling by factor 2). High-order candidates ($p+q > P^ *= 2/eta$, predicted irrelevant by Sec. 5.2) must decay; low-order candidates must persist**.

**3. Cross-modality RG (v1.1 addition):** Transform the data to a coarser representation (e.g., high-frame-rate video $\to$ gaze-tracked rhythms; EEG $\to$ envelope-only). Accepted locks must survive this lossy compression.

### Decision Rule

If **any** of the following occur, **reject** the lock:

- Test-set $K < \tau'$ (overfitting);
- High-order lock persists under coarse-graining (violates LOW);
- Low-order lock vanishes under coarse-graining (violates LOW);
- Cross-modality compression destroys the lock (observer-specific, violates **A3**).

### Rationale

E4 enforces three requirements:

- **Generalization** (temporal hold-out);
- **RG consistency** (coarse-graining decay pattern);
- **Modality independence** (survives information loss).

Together, these ensure that locks are **robust, scale-invariant, and physically real**.

# 7. The Delta Coupling Operator (DCO)

## 7.1 Abstract Definition

Let $\mathcal{P} = \{(\theta_a, \rho_a, \omega_a)\}_{a \in A}$ be the observed phases on an analysis window.

With preregistered hyperparameters $(P, lambda, gamma, texttt{accept_R}, texttt{min_persist_cycles}, text{null families}, w_bullet)$, define the **Delta Coupling Operator**:

$$
\mathrm{DCO}: \mathcal{P} \mapsto \bigl( \mathcal{L}, G^{\text{primary}}, \mathsf{RG}, \Delta H^*, \Pi, \mathrm{DeltaScore}, \text{ObserverSwap}, \chi \bigr).
$$

### Output Components

**1. Locks** $\mathcal{L}$

A list of accepted tuples:

$$
[(a, b), (p{:}q), K, \text{detune}, C_{ab}, \text{persistence}],
$$

ranked by $K$ and null-adjusted significance.

**2. Primary Graph** $G^{\text{primary}}$

Edges $(a,b)$ annotated with accepted ratio $(p{:}q)$.

**Direction:** Edges are **undirected by default**. Direction is added **only if**:

- **E3** confirms directed causality (nudging $a$ affects $b$ more than vice versa), **or**
- Exogenous directionality is known (e.g., driver-response experimental setup).

Otherwise, report as undirected (coherence without implied causation).

**3. RG Summary** $\mathsf{RG}$

Flags indicating:

- **Low-order persistence:** Do $p+q \le P/2$ candidates survive coarse-graining?
- **High-order decay:** Do $p+q > P/2$ candidates decay under coarse-graining?
- **Consistency score:** $in [0,1]$, quantifying agreement with predicted RG flow (Sec. 5.2).

**4. Harmony Gain** $\Delta H^*$

Measured improvement when enacting the **best steer plan** (below) vs. null controllers:

$$
\Delta H^* = H^*(\omega + \delta\omega^*) - H^*(\omega).
$$

Compare to:

- Random nudges (same budget $epsilon$, random directions);
- No intervention (baseline).

**5. Steer Plan** $\Pi$

Minimal-budget frequency adjustments $\{\delta\omega_a\}$ with $\sum_a |\delta\omega_a| \le \epsilon$ that:

- Reduce detuning $|\delta\omega^*|$ for the top-ranked lock;
- Increase harmony $H^*$ (predicted via linearization, validated in E3).

**Safety constraint (v1.1 addition):** Only enact $\Pi$ if a **Control Barrier Function** (CBF) holds:

$$
\mathbb{E}[ h(x_{t+1}) - h(x_t) \mid \Pi ] \ge -\alpha h(x_t),
$$

where $h$ is a preregistered safety metric (domain-specific, e.g., amplitude bounds, stability margin). If violated, project $\Pi$ to the nearest safe steer or abstain.

**6. DeltaScore**

A preregistered composite:

$$
\mathrm{DeltaScore} = \sigma\!\Big( \alpha_1 \max|K| + \alpha_2 \Delta H^* + \alpha_3 \mathbb{1}\{\text{RG low-persist}\} + \alpha_4 \mathrm{OOS\text{-}R^2} - \alpha_5 \mathrm{MDL} \Big),
$$

where:

- $\sigma$ is the logistic function (squashes to $[0,1]$);
- $\alpha_\bullet$ are preregistered weights;
- $\mathrm{OOS\text{-}R^2}$ is out-of-sample explained variance (E4 test set);
- $\mathrm{MDL}$ is the description length (number of accepted locks $\times$ log complexity).

**Interpretation:** DeltaScore aggregates: lock strength, harmony improvement, RG consistency, generalization, and parsimony. Higher is better. Typical acceptance: DeltaScore $ge 0.7$.

**7. ObserverSwap (v1.1 addition)**

Pass/fail flags and $\Delta K$ values under instrument/platform/modality swaps (E2 requirement). Reported as:

$$
\text{ObserverSwap} = \{ (\text{swap type}, \text{pass/fail}, \Delta K, \text{CI}) \}.
$$

**8. Lock-Churn** $\chi$ (v1.1 addition)

Rate of primary lock switches per pair/system (Sec. 3.3). Reported as:

$$
\chi = \frac{\# \text{ switches}}{\text{analysis window duration}}.
$$

# 9. Two Compact Theorems

## Theorem 1 (RG Irrelevance of High Order / Detune)

**Statement:**

In the dynamics with locking potential $V_{\text{lock}}$ encoded by the kernel (Sec. 3.4) and RG flow

$$
\frac{dK}{d\ell} = (2 - \Delta_{p:q}) K - \Lambda K^3 + \cdots,
$$

there exist $P^*$ and $\delta^* > 0$ such that:

If $p+q > P^*$ **or** $Big| frac{omega_b}{omega_a} - frac{p}{q} Big| > delta^*$, then* $\Delta_{p:q} > 2$ *and the unique stable fixed point is $K^* = 0$.

Hence, **only low-order, near-tuned locks persist** under noise and coarse-graining.

**Proof Sketch:**

$\Delta_{p:q} = d + \eta(p+q) + \zeta |\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q|$ increases monotonically in both $p+q$ and detune.

For sufficiently large values:

$$
2 - \Delta_{p:q} < 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{dK}{d\ell} = (\text{negative}) \cdot K - \Lambda K^3.
$$

The linear term drives $K to 0$; the cubic term stabilizes zero as the unique attractor.

Full proof in **Appendix B**.

## Theorem 2 (Monotone Harmony Under Greedy Steering)

**Statement:**

Given:

- Small intervention budget $sum_a |deltaomega_a| le epsilon$;
- Twice-differentiable $H^*$ with local Lipschitz gradient;
- Top-scoring lock $(a^*, b^*, p^*, q^*)$ with detuning $deltaomega^*$,

the **greedy steer plan** that moves $\omega$ in the direction of the lock (closest detune, largest null-adjusted $K$) satisfies:

$$
H^*(\omega + \delta\omega^*) - H^*(\omega) > 0
$$

for sufficiently small $\epsilon > 0$ (within the Lipschitz radius).

I.e., **harmony increases monotonically** until detune hits zero or budget saturates.

**Proof Sketch:**

Linearize $H^*$ in $omega$:

$$
H^*(\omega + \delta\omega) \approx H^*(\omega) + \nabla_\omega H^* \cdot \delta\omega.
$$

The greedy steer chooses $\delta\omega^*$ to maximize $\nabla_\omega H^* \cdot \delta\omega$ subject to $|deltaomega| le epsilon$.

By construction of $H^*$ (Sec. 3.3), the gradient $\nabla_\omega H^*$ points toward reduced detuning and increased coherence.

Lipschitzness bounds curvature, ensuring first-order ascent dominates within budget.

# 11. Limitations and Failure Modes

HSM is **deliberately falsifiable**. Failure modes are **features, not bugs**‚Äîthey tell us when the method does not apply.

---

## 11.1 Nonstationarity

**Problem:** Rapid drift in $\omega$ can mimic detuning or create spurious high-order locks.

**Mitigation:**

- Use shorter analysis windows ($T downarrow$);
- Adaptive frequency tracking (e.g., sliding-window STFT);
- Block-wise stationarity test (Appendix K of LOW[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Low-Order-Wins-A-Renormalization-Group-Principle-for-Integer-Relation-Persistence-f5577d610f8c4470a8160254a620751c?pvs=21)).

**Failure signature:** E4 temporal hold-out fails (train/test $K$ values uncorrelated).

---

## 11.2 Aliasing / Sampling

**Problem:** Insufficient sampling rate $f_s$ folds high-frequency content into low frequencies, creating false integer ratios.

**Mitigation:**

- Verify Nyquist: $f_s ge 2 cdot (p omega_b)_{max}$;
- Anti-aliasing filter before downsampling;
- Aliasing audit (Appendix G of LOW[[1]](https://www.notion.so/Low-Order-Wins-A-Renormalization-Group-Principle-for-Integer-Relation-Persistence-f5577d610f8c4470a8160254a620751c?pvs=21)).

**Failure signature:** E2 symmetry breaks under platform swap (high-$f_s$ vs. low-$f_s$ platforms yield different $(p,q)$).

---

## 11.3 Octave Confusion

**Problem:** Harmonics shadow primaries (e.g., $4{:}2$ vs. $2{:}1$).

**Mitigation:**

- Identifiability constraint (one primary per octave band per pair);
- Prefer smallest coprime $(p,q)$ if multiple candidates;
- Banded analysis (report locks per frequency band).

**Failure signature:** Multiple $(p,q)$ accepted in same band (violates identifiability).

---

## 11.4 Short Records

**Problem:** Too few cycles inflate false positives (noise masquerades as locks).

**Mitigation:**

- Enforce $\texttt{min\_persist\_cycles} \ge 8$ (preregistered);
- Require $T \ge 10 \cdot (2\pi / \omega_{\min})$ (at least 10 periods of slowest oscillation).

**Failure signature:** E4 out-of-sample fails (locks don't replicate on test set).

---

## 11.5 Symmetry Breaks from Preprocessing

**Problem:** Unregistered preprocessing (e.g., forward-only causal filters, asymmetric windowing) injects directionality or breaks gauge invariance.

**Mitigation:**

- Use zero-phase filters (e.g., `filtfilt` in MATLAB/Python);
- Preregister all preprocessing;
- E2 guards this by testing symmetry explicitly.

**Failure signature:** E2 time-reversal check fails (undirected measure changes sign).

# Appendix A: Proof of the Clock Compiler's Validity

## A.1 Event-Train Renewal Clock

**Claim:** For point-process data ${t_1, t_2, ldots}$, the renewal clock $\theta(t) = 2\pi (t - t_k)/(t_{k+1} - t_k)$ for $t \in [t_k, t_{k+1})$ satisfies **A1 (Vibration)**.

**Proof:**

1. **Phase survives amplitude mute:** Events $\{t_k\}$ are binary (spike/no-spike), independent of amplitude. Muting amplitude (setting spike heights to zero) does not destroy event times. Phase is recoverable from $\{t_k\}$ alone.
2. **Time-reversal:** Under $t to -t$, event sequence reverses: ${t_1, t_2, ldots} to {-t_n, ldots, -t_2, -t_1}$. Undirected measures (ISI distribution, phase coherence magnitude) are preserved; directed measures (spike-triggered average) reverse sign.
3. **Frequency:** Instantaneous frequency $\omega(t) = \dot{\theta}(t) = 2\pi/(t_{k+1} - t_k)$ is well-defined (inverse of inter-event interval).

Hence, $(\theta, \omega)$ from renewal clock is admissible. $\square$

---

## A.2 Semantic Template Clock

**Claim:** For symbolic stream $m(t)$ with template basis ${T_k}$, defining $\theta_{\text{tpl}}(t)$ as the phase in the best-matching template cycle satisfies **A1** if templates are preregistered and operationally defined.

**Proof:**

1. **Phase survives amplitude mute:** Templates are defined on **semantic content** (text, images, actions), not amplitude. "Muting amplitude" corresponds to e.g., stripping formatting but retaining words. If template matching survives this transformation (E1 test), phase is operational.
2. **Time-reversal:** Semantic streams often lack natural time-reversal symmetry (narratives have directionality). HSM allows this: if the system is **known** to be directed, E1 time-reversal is relaxed. Otherwise, undirected template phases (e.g., daily cycles) must satisfy E1.
3. **Frequency:** "Instantaneous frequency" is the rate of template cycling (e.g., templates/day for weekly ritual).

Hence, preregistered semantic clocks are admissible. $\square$

# Appendix B: Proofs of Theorems 1‚Äì2

## B.1 Theorem 1 (RG Irrelevance)

**Restatement:**

Under RG flow $frac{dK}{dell} = (2 - Delta_{p:q}) K - Lambda K^3$, with $Delta_{p:q} = d + eta(p+q) + zeta |omega_b/omega_a - p/q|$, there exist $P^*$ and $\delta^* > 0$ such that if $p+q > P^*$ or $|omega_b/omega_a - p/q| > delta^*$, then $K^* = 0$ is the unique stable fixed point.

**Proof:**

Fixed points satisfy:

$$
(2 - \Delta_{p:q}) K^* - \Lambda (K^*)^3 = 0.
$$

Solutions: $K^* = 0$ or $K^* = \pm \sqrt{(2 - \Delta_{p:q})/\Lambda}$ (latter requires $Delta_{p:q} < 2$).

**Case 1:** $Delta_{p:q} > 2$.

Then $(2 - Delta_{p:q}) < 0$, and the only real fixed point is $K^* = 0$.

Stability: For small perturbation $K = epsilon$,

$$
\frac{dK}{d\ell} = (2 - \Delta_{p:q}) \epsilon - \Lambda \epsilon^3 \approx (\text{negative}) \cdot \epsilon \quad \Rightarrow \quad K \to 0.
$$

Hence $K^* = 0$ is **stable**.

**Case 2:** $Delta_{p:q} < 2$.

Non-zero fixed points exist: $K^* = pm sqrt{(2 - Delta_{p:q})/Lambda}$.

Stability: Linearize around $K^* > 0$:

$$
\frac{d(K - K^*)}{d\ell} \approx -(2 - \Delta_{p:q}) (K - K^*) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{stable}.
$$

Zero is **unstable** in this regime.

**Critical order** $P^*$ **and detune $delta^*$:**

Since $\Delta_{p:q} = d + \eta(p+q) + \zeta |\omega_b/\omega_a - p/q|$ increases monotonically in both $p+q$ and detune, choose:

$$
P^* := \frac{2 - d}{\eta}, \quad \delta^* := \frac{2 - d - \eta P}{\zeta}.
$$
