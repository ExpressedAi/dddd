# 2. First Principles

## 2.1 Objects and Claims

* **Primitive.** A stubborn, nudge-resistant equilibrium (phase-structured but non-steerable at our budget).
* **Law.** A null-beating, steer-responsive, RG-stable deviation that survives coarse-graining and passes E-audits.
* **Null ( \mathcal G ).** Broadband phases with no persistent small-integer structure; all reported structure must beat this.

## 2.2 Phasor Model

Let signals/modes (i) be phasors (x_i = A_i e^{i\theta_i}) with frequency (f_i) and damping (\Gamma_i) ((Q_i=f_i/\Gamma_i)).

[

[

]
**Eligibility** requires (\varepsilon^{p:q}*{ab}>0). Detune signal:
[

**Prediction:** integer-thinning‚Äî(\log|K_{p:q}|) decreases approximately linearly with ((p+q)) after detune normalization.

K_{p:q} ;\propto; \left|;\left\langle e^{,i,\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a)}\right\rangle_w\right|
\times \sqrt{Q_a Q_b};\times;\mathrm{gain}(A_a, A_b),
]
with a fixed, declared gain function (e.g., normalized by (\sum A)).

**Eligibility and detune.**
[
\varepsilon^{p:q}*{ab}=\big[2\pi K*{p:q}-(\Gamma_a+\Gamma_b)\big]*+,\qquad
s_f=\varepsilon^{p:q}*{ab}(p f_a-q f_b).
]

**Coherence and contributions.**
[
X=\sum_i A_i e^{i\theta_i},\quad T=\frac{|X|}{\sum A_i},\quad C_i=\cos(\theta_i-\arg X)-T.
]

**Brittleness.**
[
\zeta=\frac{\Gamma_a p^2+\Gamma_b q^2}{\max{\varepsilon^{p:q}*{ab},,K*{p:q}}}\quad\text{(or replace numerator by measured phase diffusion)}.
]

# 6. Sore-Thumb Score (S^*)

## 6.1 Purpose

Unified, audit-gated evidence that a small-integer lock deviates from the LLN-null, is steer-responsive, and survives coarse-graining.

## 6.2 Definition

[
S^* ;=; w_Z,|Z| ;+; w_\chi,\tilde{\chi}^2 ;+; w_{KL},D_{KL} ;+; w_K,|K|
]
with nonnegative weights (w_\bullet). (S^*) is **informative only if audits E0‚ÄìE2 pass**; promotion to Law requires E3‚ÄìE4 passes on held-out windows.

### Components (window-local)

* **(|Z|)**: standardized excess of lock-consistent statistics over null.
* **(\tilde{\chi}^2)**: goodness-of-fit residual for phase histograms vs null (unit-mean when null).
* **(D_{KL})**: Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence between observed and null distributions of lock features.
* **(|K|)**: aggregate lock strength (rank-weighted or sum over accepted (K_{p:q})).

## 6.3 Inputs

Windowed phasors ({A_i,\theta_i,f_i,\Gamma_i}_i), candidate ratios (R_L), null generators (shuffles/surrogates), audit statuses, calibration constants.

## 6.4 Estimators

### Z-score (lock-consistent)

Choose a sufficient statistic (U) (e.g., mean resultant length of (e^{i e_\phi}), or triad variant). Estimate null mean/variance from surrogates:
[
Z ;=; \frac{U - \mu_{\text{null}}}{\sigma_{\text{null}}+ \epsilon_Z},\quad \epsilon_Z>0.
]
Use the same windowing, gauges, and acceptance masks in both real and null pipelines.

### (\tilde{\chi}^2) (phase distribution)

Partition (e_\phi\in(-\pi,\pi]) into (B) bins. Compare observed counts (n_b) to expected (E_b) under null:
[
\chi^2=\sum_{b=1}^{B}\frac{(n_b-E_b)^2}{E_b+\epsilon_\chi},\quad
\tilde{\chi}^2 = \frac{\chi^2}{\mathbb{E}*{\text{null}}[\chi^2]+\epsilon*\chi}.
]
(\tilde{\chi}^2\approx 1) at null; (>1) indicates structure.

### (D_{KL}) (distributional lift)

Smooth counts to probabilities (p_b, q_b) (lock vs null) with additive (\epsilon_{KL}):
[
D_{KL}(p\Vert q)=\sum_{b=1}^B p_b \log\frac{p_b}{q_b+\epsilon_{KL}}.
]

### (|K|) (accepted locks)

Aggregate per-ratio strengths with MDL weights:
[
|K| ;=; \sum_{(p:q)\in R_L} \underbrace{\varpi_{p:q}}*{\text{e.g., } \propto \tfrac{1}{p+q}};\hat K*{p:q};\mathbf{1}{\text{ratio accepted}}.
]
Use a **phase-aligned** (\hat K_{p:q} \propto \big|\langle e^{i,\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a)}\rangle\big| \sqrt{Q_aQ_b}) with declared gain.

## 6.5 Gating by Audits

* **E0 (Calibration):** LLN, p-values, windows, gauges OK ‚Üí else (S^*) not reported.
* **E1 (Vibration):** narrowband, phase survives amplitude mute ‚Üí else demote.
* **E2 (Symmetry):** invariance to declared gauge/permutation ‚Üí else void.
* **E3 (Causal):** small on-manifold nudges increase (K,\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},T) with correct lag/sign ‚Üí required for Law.
* **E4 (RG):** √ó2 coarse-grain preserves low-order hierarchy (integer-thinning) ‚Üí required for Law.

## 6.6 Weights and Calibration

* Normalize each component to a **unit scale at null** (median or mean to 1).
* Default: (w_Z=w_{KL}=w_K=1,; w_\chi=1). Adjust only via pre-registered protocol.
* Report sensitivity: (\partial S^*/\partial w_\bullet) over a small simplex.

## 6.7 Robustness Protocols

### Surrogates (null family)

* **Phase shuffle** within windows.
* **Spectrum-preserving detune** (destroy integer relations, keep amplitudes).
* **High-order-only controls** (match energy, remove low-order).

### Cross-checks

* **Time reversal** (directed terms flip sign only).
* **Amplitude mute** (phase-only coherence remains if genuine).
* **Gauge flips / permutations** (invariant (S^*)).

### Multiplicity control

* FDR control on multiple ratios/windows; report q-values.
* Minimum trials for E3: ‚â•240 (¬±/‚àí/sham split), (p<0.01) after FDR, (Z\ge3).

## 6.8 Stability, Brittleness, and Confounds

* Always co-report (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, \varepsilon_{\text{stab}}, \zeta).
* De-emphasize (|K|) when (\zeta) is high or (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}\le0).
* Treat amplitude-only boosts without phase evidence as confounds (mute test).

## 6.9 RG Signature (Integer-Thinning)

* Plot detune-normalized (\log \hat K_{p:q}) vs (p+q).
* Accept only if slope (<0) and persists under √ó2 pooling.
* Record survivors‚Äô order set; demote any claim where high-order outruns low-order.

## 6.10 Decision Thresholds (defaults)

* **Promote to Primitive:** (S^*>S_{\min}) and E0‚ÄìE2 pass; E3/E4 pending.
* **Promote to Law:** E0‚ÄìE4 pass, integer-thinning holds, (S^*>S_{\law}) on held-out windows.
* Suggested: (S_{\min}=2.0) (unit scale), (S_{\law}=3.0), configurable.

## 6.11 Computation Recipe (window (w))

**Inputs:** data ({A_i,\theta_i,f_i,\Gamma_i}), (R_L), surrogates (S), audits.
**Outputs:** (S^*), components, Œµ/Œ∂ maps, decision flags.

**Steps**

1. Build surrogates with matched windows/gauges; compute (\mu_{\text{null}},\sigma_{\text{null}}), (E_b), (q_b).
2. For each ((a,b,p{:}q)\in R_L): compute (e_\phi, \hat K_{p:q}, \varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, s_f, D_\phi, \zeta).
3. Select accepted ratios by eligibility (Œµ_cap>0), phase tolerance, amplitude-lock, and audits-in-the-loop.
4. Compute (U) and (Z); histogram (e_\phi) for (\tilde{\chi}^2); smooth to (p_b) for (D_{KL}).
5. Aggregate (|K|) with MDL weights; form (S^*).
6. Gate on audits; if E3 performed, update (U) pre/post-nudge; if E4, recompute on pooled windows.
7. Emit window summary and Œî-Report entries.

## 6.12 Pseudocode

```
function S_star(window_data, R_L, surrogates, audits):

    null_stats ‚Üê calibrate_from(surrogates)  // Œº_null, œÉ_null, E_b, q_b
    K_sum ‚Üê 0; accepted ‚Üê []
    for (a,b,p:q) in R_L:

        Khat ‚Üê |‚ü®e^{i eœÜ}‚ü©| * sqrt(Q_a Q_b) * gain(A_a,A_b)
        Œµcap ‚Üê max(0, 2œÄKhat - (Œì_a + Œì_b))

```

## 6.13 Reporting Block (Œî-Report)

* (S^*) and components with CIs (bootstrap over frames).
* Accepted ratio list with (K, \varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, \varepsilon_{\text{stab}}, \zeta).
* Integer-thinning plot and pooled (√ó2) results.
* Surrogate specs, sample sizes, FDR details.
* E3 micro-nudge telemetry (pre/post), E4 persistence verdicts.

## 6.14 Failure Modes (demote/void)

* Surrogate replication of effects; (\tilde{\chi}^2\approx1), (D_{KL}\approx0), (Z\approx0).
* Gauge dependence; amplitude-only artifacts; lack of RG persistence.
* Phase forcing outside capture ((|s_f|>1)).
* Non-monotone or absent integer-thinning after normalization.

## 6.15 Defaults (safe)

* (B=24) phase bins; (\epsilon_Z=\epsilon_\chi=\epsilon_{KL}=10^{-9}).
* Gain((A_a,A_b)=\frac{A_a A_b}{(\sum_i A_i)^2}) (declare and keep fixed).
* MDL weight (\varpi_{p:q} \propto 1/(p+q)) (normalize to (\sum\varpi=1)).
* Bootstrap 1000 resamples for CIs; report medians and IQRs.

# 7. Œî-Coupling Operator (DCO++)

## 7.1 Purpose

End-to-end orchestration of **detection ‚Üí adjudication ‚Üí control ‚Üí proof** for small-integer locks. Produces a minimal low-order lock cover, a steer plan, and a Œî-Report with audits.

## 7.2 Inputs

* Windowed phasors ({A_i,\theta_i,f_i,\Gamma_i}_i).
* Candidate ratios (R_L) and tolerances ((\tau_f,\phi_{\text{tol}},\phi_{\text{snap}},W)).
* Audit config (E0‚ÄìE4), null generators, observer budgets, anneal schedule.
* MDL weights (\varpi_{p:q}), Harmony parameters ((\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta)).

## 7.3 Outputs

* **Lock cover** (\mathcal L): accepted ratios with ((K,\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},\varepsilon_{\text{stab}},\zeta)).
* **Steer plan** (\Pi): ordered actions on F/P/A/S axes with budgets.
* **Œî-Report** block: (S^*) components, Œµ/Œ∂ maps, audits, integer-thinning, RG verdicts, PCO fields.
* **Telemetry**: nudges, lags, reversions, anneal logs.

## 7.4 Core computations

**Phase error & eligibility**
[
e_\phi=\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a),\quad
\varepsilon^{p:q}=\big[2\pi K_{p:q}-(\Gamma_a+\Gamma_b)\big]_+,\quad
s_f=\varepsilon^{p:q}(p f_a-q f_b).
]

**Stability & brittleness**
[
\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}=\varepsilon^{p:q}-\kappa,\mathrm{Var}(e_\phi),\qquad
\zeta=\frac{\Gamma_a p^2+\Gamma_b q^2}{\max(\varepsilon^{p:q},K_{p:q})}.
]

**Harmony**
[
X=\sum_i A_i e^{i\theta_i},;; T=\frac{|X|}{\sum A_i},;;
C_i=\cos(\theta_i-\arg X)-T.
]

## 7.5 Priority & gating

* **Order:** Frequency ‚Üí Phase ‚Üí Amplitude ‚Üí Space.
* **Gates:** (i) (|s_f|\le1) before any phase trim; (ii) (|e_\phi|\le\phi_{\text{tol}}) before amplitude reweight; (iii) audits-in-the-loop at each acceptance.

## 7.6 Algorithm (pseudocode)


## 7.7 Acceptance rules

A candidate ((a,b,p{:}q)) is **accepted** iff:

1. (\varepsilon^{p:q}>0) and (|s_f|\le\tau_f);
2. (|e_\phi|\le\phi_{\text{tol}}) after at most one Snap(_\phi);
3. Amplitude-Lock holds (W) windows;
4. E1 & E2 pass;
5. If executed, E3 shows on-manifold lift vs null/sham;
6. E4 preserves low-order thinning hierarchy.

## 7.8 Space operator (bridges)

* **Add** composite edges that **reduce** ((p+q)) and (\zeta) while increasing (ŒîH^*).
* **Prune** edges with (dK/d\ell<0), persistent detune, or gauge dependence.
* **Hysteresis:** once leadership changes, require an E3-verified advantage to revert.

## 7.9 Complexity & budgets

* Pairwise scan (O(|R_L|,M^2)) for (M) modes; pruning reduces to near-linear per window in practice.
* Audits: E3 requires ‚â•240 trials split (+/‚àí/sham). Record wall-clock and sample budgets; throttle to corridor ([K_{\min},K_{\max}]).

## 7.10 Telemetry & PCO

* Log: snaps (axis, magnitude, time), holds/releases, nudges, lag estimates, reversions, pool factors.
* PCO: seed, code rev, config hash, data hash, window ids, surrogate specs.

## 7.11 Failure modes (kill-switches)

* Any phase action with (|s_f|>1) ‚Üí revert; mark candidate invalid for window.
* Amplitude boosts without phase evidence ‚Üí mute & demote.
* Loss of symmetry invariance ‚Üí void.
* RG failure (no thinning/persistence) ‚Üí demote to Primitive/Probe.

## 7.12 Œî-Report fields (emitted by DCO++)

* E-audit outcomes (E0‚ÄìE4), micro-nudge telemetry.
* Steer plan (\Pi) (F/P/A/S), expected (+ŒîH^*), observer & anneal budgets.

# 8. Unified Polarity Control (Frequency ‚Üí Phase ‚Üí Amplitude ‚Üí Space)

## 8.1 Purpose

Single controller stack that lands and certifies small-integer locks with minimal interventions. Priority order is strict: **Frequency (eligibility) ‚Üí Phase (timing) ‚Üí Amplitude (harmony) ‚Üí Space (support)**. One Snap per axis per window. Re-measure between axes.

## 8.2 Signals

For candidate ((a,b,p{:}q)):
[
e_\phi=\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a),\quad s_\phi=\sin e_\phi,\quad
\varepsilon=\big[2\pi K_{p:q}-(\Gamma_a+\Gamma_b)\big]*+,
]
[
s_f=\varepsilon,(p f_a-q f_b),\qquad
X=\sum_i A_i e^{i\theta_i},\quad T=\frac{|X|}{\sum A_i},\quad
C_i=\cos(\theta_i-\arg X)-T.
]
Stability/brittleness:
[
\varepsilon*{\text{stab}}=\varepsilon-\kappa,\mathrm{Var}(e_\phi),\qquad
\zeta=\frac{\Gamma_a p^2+\Gamma_b q^2}{\max(\varepsilon, K_{p:q})}.
]

## 8.3 Axis F ‚Äî Frequency (eligibility)

**Goal.** Ensure on-manifold timing is meaningful before any phase action.

**Rules.**

* **No phase forcing when (|s_f|>1).** Only eligibility edits are allowed.
* **Snap(_f).** Trigger if (|s_f|>1) or (d|s_f|/dt>0) despite polishing; action: tiny retune (\delta f/f\le2%) or widen Œµ by declared optics (band, window).
* **Release(_f).** When (|s_f|\le\tau_f) (default (0.2)).

**Acceptance.** Move to Phase only if (\varepsilon>0) and (|s_f|\le\tau_f).

## 8.4 Axis P ‚Äî Phase (timing)

**Goal.** Align phases on the eligible manifold.

**Rules.**

* **Low-gain loop.** (\delta\omega=-k_\ell s_\phi) with (k_\ell) small and bounded.
* **Snap(_\phi).** One-time trim (5^\circ!-!15^\circ) if (|e_\phi|>\phi_{\text{snap}}) (default (25^\circ)) or imminent slip (predicted by sign of (s_\phi \dot e_\phi)).
* **Release(_\phi).** Hold when (|e_\phi|\le\phi_{\text{tol}}) (default (10^\circ)).

**Guards.**

* If (\varepsilon\to0) or (|s_f|\uparrow), back to Frequency.
* Record lag and overshoot; abort if oscillatory beyond corridor.

## 8.5 Axis A ‚Äî Amplitude (harmony)

**Goal.** Concentrate weight on coherent contributors without manufacturing phase artifacts.

**Update.**
[
A_i \leftarrow \Pi_{\Delta}!\big(A_i,\exp(\beta C_i)\big),\quad \beta\in[0,0.05],
]
project onto the simplex (\sum_i A_i=\text{const}).

**Stop (Amplitude-Lock).** For (W=3) windows:
[
\max_i|C_i|\le\tau_C=0.02,\quad |\Delta A|_1\le\tau_A=0.02\sum A,\quad \Delta T\le\tau_T=10^{-3}.
]

**Guards.**

* If (|e_\phi|) drifts beyond (\phi_{\text{tol}}), revert last amplitude step and return to Phase.
* Mute-test: if phase evidence vanishes under amplitude mute, demote.

## 8.6 Axis S ‚Äî Space (support/topology)

**Goal.** Edit graph/tuples to reduce ((p+q)) and (\zeta), raise (ŒîH^*), and preserve audits.

**Edits.**

* **Bridges.** Propose composite edges that **reduce** order and detune while increasing (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}).
* **Prune.** Remove edges with (dK/d\ell<0), persistent detune, or gauge dependence.
* **Hysteresis.** Leadership changes require E3 advantage; no flip-flop on correlation.

# 9. Capture, Stability, Brittleness (Œµ_cap, Œµ_stab, Œ∂)

## 9.1 Purpose

Quantify when a candidate lock is **eligible** (can be captured), **stable** (resists calibrated perturbations), and **robust** (low brittleness). These fields are mandatory for adjudication, control, and promotion to Law.

## 9.2 Capture Bandwidth ( \varepsilon_{\text{cap}} )

**Definition (pair (a,b), ratio (p{:}q)).**
[
\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q} ;=; \big[,2\pi K_{p:q}-(\Gamma_a+\Gamma_b),\big]*+,
]
where (K*{p:q}) is coupling (pull, rad/s), (\Gamma) are dampings. Units: Hz (via (2\pi)).

**Interpretation.** Minimum detune (|p f_a - q f_b|) that can be corrected at current pull and damping. If (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}=0), phase control is forbidden.

**Measurement.**

1. Estimate (K_{p:q}) by phase-aligned resultant:
   [
   \hat K_{p:q} ;\propto; \left|\left\langle e^{,i,\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a)}\right\rangle\right|
   \sqrt{Q_a Q_b};\mathrm{gain}(A_a,A_b).
   ]
2. Compute (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) and the **eligibility signal**:
   [
   s_f=\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q},(p f_a-q f_b).
   ]
3. Gate: only (|s_f|\le \tau_f) and (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0) advance to Phase.

**Controls affecting (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}).**

* ‚Üë(K_{p:q}) via micro-phase polishing on-manifold.
* ‚Üì(\Gamma) via denoising/averaging (observer budgeting).
* Small frequency retunes to reduce (|p f_a-q f_b|) into the capture window.

## 9.3 Stability Window ( \varepsilon_{\text{stab}} )

**Definition (operational proxy).**
[
\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}^{p:q} ;=; \varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q} ;-; \kappa,\mathrm{Var}*w(e*\phi),\qquad
e_\phi=\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a),;\kappa>0.
]
(\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}>0) indicates the lock remains captured under calibrated fluctuations.

**Calibration.**

* Choose (\kappa) so that (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}\approx0) at the **break-even** noise level where slips begin (measured by controlled phase jitter injections).
* Report (\kappa) and injected noise budgets in Œî-Report.

**Perturbation protocol (E3 context).**

* Inject micro-nudges: ¬±5¬∞ phase or ¬±2% frequency, on-manifold.
* Record (\Delta e_\phi), recovery lag, and post-nudge (\mathrm{Var}(e_\phi)).
* Stability passes if (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}>0) and recovery is monotone within corridor.

**Failure signature.**

* (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0) but (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}\le0): fragile or borderline lock; demote or re-optics.

## 9.4 Brittleness ( \zeta )

**Definition (sensitivity index).**
[
\zeta ;=;
\frac{D_\phi}{\max{\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q},,K_{p:q}}},
\qquad
D_\phi=\Gamma_a p^2+\Gamma_b q^2
\quad\text{or}\quad
D_\phi=\mathrm{Var}*w(\Delta e*\phi)/\Delta t.
]
Dimensionless. High (\zeta) = small disturbances cause outsized phase error; low (\zeta) = robust.

**Roles.**

* Penalized explicitly in (H^*) (Œ©* objective).
* Used to prune candidates even if (K) appears large (reject brittle high-order lookalikes).
* Drives **integer-thinning** interpretation: at fixed detune, (\zeta) rises with ((p+q)).

**Measurement notes.**

* Prefer data-driven (D_\phi) when diffusion can be estimated; otherwise use damping proxy.
* Always co-report ((K,\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},\varepsilon_{\text{stab}})) alongside (\zeta).

## 9.5 Joint Geometry (trade-offs)

* **Capture vs Stability.** Increasing (K) (via phase polish) can raise both (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) and (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}); however, if polishing pushes amplitude weighting aggressively, (D_\phi) may increase ‚Üí (\zeta\uparrow).
* **Brittleness vs MDL.** For ties in (S^*), prefer the lower ((p+q)) **and** lower (\zeta); prune higher-order even if (K) is slightly larger.
* **Observer budget.** Larger windows ‚Üì(\Gamma) (variance), ‚Üë(\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}), but risk nonstationarity; obey (\Delta\omega,\Delta t\gtrsim c).

## 9.6 Tuning & Defaults

* (\tau_f=0.2); (\phi_{\text{tol}}=10^\circ); (\phi_{\text{snap}}=25^\circ); (W=3).
* E3 micro-nudge: ¬±5¬∞ or ¬±2% with ‚â•240 trials, FDR (p<0.01), (Z\ge3).
* Start (\kappa) from the slope linking (\mathrm{Var}(e_\phi)) to slip probability (fit on surrogates + pilot data).

## 9.7 Practical Estimators

**A. Capture sweep (detune test).**

1. With Phase held, sweep small (\delta f) around on-manifold.
2. Record the largest (|p f_a - q f_b|) that maintains (|e_\phi|\le\phi_{\text{tol}}) without a snap.
3. This empirical width estimates (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) directly.

**B. Stability via jitter map.**

1. Inject band-limited phase noise (\eta_\phi) with RMS grid ({0,\sigma_1,\dots}).
2. For each level, estimate (\mathrm{Var}(e_\phi)) and slip frequency.
3. Find (\sigma^\star) where slips begin; set (\kappa) so that (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}(\sigma^\star)\approx0).

**C. Brittleness from diffusion.**
[
D_\phi \approx \frac{1}{\Delta t}\sum_{k}(e_{\phi,k+1}-e_{\phi,k})^2,
\quad
\zeta = \frac{D_\phi}{\max(\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},K)}.
]

## 9.8 Controller Hooks

**Frequency axis (F).**

* If (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}=0) or (|s_f|>1): **no** phase action; retune ‚â§2% or widen Œµ.

**Phase axis (P).**

* Execute low-gain loop only with (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0); one snap allowed if (|e_\phi|>\phi_{\text{snap}}).
* Monitor (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}); if it turns ‚â§0, revert last phase edit.

**Amplitude axis (A).**

* MWU steps increase (T) but must not raise (\zeta) beyond corridor; revert if (\zeta) spikes or (e_\phi) drifts.

**Space axis (S).**

* Prefer bridges that **raise** (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}), **increase** (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}), and **lower** (\zeta).
* Prune edges with (dK/d\ell<0) or chronically high (\zeta).

## 9.9 Audits & Decisions

**E1 (vibration).**

* Report (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) baseline; amplitude mute must not erase phase evidence for accepted locks.

**E3 (causal micro-nudge).**

* Success = on-manifold nudges increase (K), (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}), (T) **and** keep (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}>0).
* Sham/off-manifold nudges produce no systematic lift.

**E4 (RG).**

* Under √ó2 pooling: (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) for low-order stays >0; (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}) does not degrade; (\zeta) does not inflate.
* High-order candidates lose (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) first (integer-thinning).

**Decision table.**

| Condition                                                                 | Action                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}=0)                                              | Reject candidate; frequency-only edits permitted    |
| (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0), (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}\le0)             | Demote to Primitive/Probe; re-optics                |
| (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0), (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}>0), high (\zeta) | Keep under watch; penalize in (H^*); do not promote |
| All three favorable                                                       | Candidate eligible for promotion (with E3/E4)       |

## 9.10 Reporting (Œî-Report fields)

* For each accepted lock: ((p{:}q), \hat K, \varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, \varepsilon_{\text{stab}}, \zeta) with CIs.
* Pre/post E3 deltas: (\Delta K, \Delta \varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, \Delta T), recovery lag.
* RG table: pooled vs unpooled (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}, \varepsilon_{\text{stab}}, \zeta).
* Corridor bounds ([K_{\min},K_{\max}]), (\kappa), noise budgets, and any reversions.

## 9.11 Failure Modes (kill-switches)

* Phase actions when (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}=0) or (|s_f|>1).
* Apparent stability built from amplitude-only gain (fails amplitude-mute).
* Gauge dependence of (\varepsilon) fields across declared permutations.
* No integer-thinning in pooled (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}) hierarchy.
* Rising (\zeta) across Œ©* steps without ŒîH* gains.

## 10.3 E0 ‚Äî Calibration (LLN, p-values, gauges)

**Protocol.**

## 10.6 E3 ‚Äî Causal Micro-Nudge (tiny, on-manifold interventions)

# 11. Harmony Optimizer & (H^*)

## 11.1 Purpose

Maximize coherent alignment while suppressing brittleness. (H^*) is the control Lyapunov objective Œ©* climbs subject to admissible transforms and audit gates.

## 11.2 Definitions

Phasors (x_i=A_i e^{i\theta_i}). Group phasor (X=\sum_i A_i e^{i\theta_i}). Coherence
[
T=\frac{|X|}{\sum_i A_i}\in[0,1],\qquad
C_i=\cos(\theta_i-\arg X)-T.
]
Let accepted candidate ratios be (\mathcal R\subset R_L) with lock kernels (K_{p:q}), captures (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q}), stabilities (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}^{p:q}), and brittleness indices (\zeta_{p:q}).

**Objective (reportable scalar):**
[
H^*(A,\theta;\mathcal R)=
\underbrace{\alpha,T}*{\text{coherence}}
;-;
\underbrace{\beta,\bar{\zeta}}*{\text{robustness}}
;+;
\underbrace{\gamma,\overline{\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}}}*{\text{stability}}
;-;
\underbrace{\delta,\overline{(p+q)}}*{\text{MDL}},
]
with window-local bars indicating averages over (\mathcal R) (MDL term uses weights (\varpi_{p:q}\propto 1/(p+q))). Defaults: (\alpha=\beta=\gamma=1,;\delta=0.2) (declare per study).

## 11.3 Constraints & Guards

* **Admissible transforms only** (A2): small frequency retunes, phase trims, amplitude reweight, topology edits within declared budgets.
* **Priority** F‚ÜíP‚ÜíA‚ÜíS: (H^*) updates on A/S are legal **only if** eligibility and phase tolerance hold.
* **Audit gates:** No (H^*) improvement is accepted if E1/E2 fail; causal lifts must be confirmed by E3 when promoting; persistence by E4.

## 11.4 Amplitude Optimizer (MWU on the simplex)

Let (A\in\Delta={A_i\ge0,\sum A_i=\text{const}}). Update by multiplicative weights:
[
A_i^{(t+1)} ;=; \Pi_{\Delta}!\left(A_i^{(t)}\exp{\beta,C_i^{(t)} - \lambda,g_i^{(t)}}\right),
]
where (\beta\in[0,0.05]) and (g_i) is a brittleness guard (e.g., gradient proxy from (\partial\zeta/\partial A_i) or a penalty if (A_i) feeds high-order candidates). (\Pi_{\Delta}) projects back to the simplex (e.g., Euclidean projection).

**Stop (Amplitude-Lock).** For (W=3) windows:
[
\max_i |C_i|\le\tau_C,\quad |\Delta A|_1\le\tau_A,\quad \Delta T\le\tau_T,
]
with defaults (\tau_C=0.02,;\tau_A=0.02\sum A,;\tau_T=10^{-3}).

**Mute test (guard).** Temporarily uniformize amplitudes; genuine phase coherence must persist. If it vanishes, revert latest amplitude edits.

## 11.5 Phase-Coupled Enhancement (safe)

When eligible ((\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0, |s_f|\le\tau_f)) and within phase tolerance:
[
\delta\omega=-k_\ell \sin e_\phi,\quad e_\phi=\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_b-q\theta_a),
]
with small (k_\ell). One Snap(*\phi) per window allowed (5‚Äì15¬∞) if (|e*\phi|>\phi_{\text{snap}}). Any phase action with (|s_f|>1) is forbidden.

## 11.6 Space Optimizer (bridge/prune)

**Bridge proposal.** Add composite edges that reduce ((p+q)) and (\zeta) while increasing (H^*) and (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}).
**Prune rule.** Remove candidates with (dK/d\ell<0), persistent detune, or gauge dependence. Leadership switches require an E3-verified (H^*) advantage (hysteresis).

## 11.7 Brittleness Handling

[
\bar{\zeta}=\sum_{(p:q)\in\mathcal R}\varpi_{p:q},\zeta_{p:q},\qquad
\zeta_{p:q}=\frac{D_\phi}{\max(\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}^{p:q},K_{p:q})}.
]

* Downweight or temporarily exclude channels with (\zeta) above corridor.
* Schedule anneal (heat/quench) if (\bar{\zeta}) plateaus while (T) grows‚Äîavoid brittle pseudo-coherence.

## 11.8 Gradient Sketch (analysis only; not required for runtime)

Let (\phi_i=\theta_i-\arg X). Then
[
\partial_{\theta_i} T ;\approx; \frac{A_i}{\sum A},\sin\phi_i,
\quad
\partial_{A_i} T ;\approx; \frac{\cos\phi_i - T}{\sum A} ;=; \frac{C_i}{\sum A}.
]
Use these to sanity-check MWU steps and phase low-gain choices. Do **not** operate with these when eligibility fails.

## 11.9 Pseudocode

```
function HarmonyStep(state):
  // Preconditions: Œµ_cap>0 and |e_œÜ|‚â§œÜ_tol for active candidates
  // A) Amplitude pass
  C ‚Üê cos(Œ∏ - argX) - T
  A ‚Üê ProjSimplex( A ‚äô exp( Œ≤*C - Œª*brittleness_grad(A) ) )
  if not amplitude_lock(): repeat up to budget; else proceed

  // B) Space pass (optional)
  B ‚Üê propose_bridges( reduce_order=true, reduce_zeta=true, ŒîH*>0 )
  if audits_pass(B): apply; else discard
  P ‚Üê propose_prunes( dK/d‚Ñì<0 or Œ∂ high or detune persistent )
  apply P if audits ok

  // C) Safeguards
  if amplitude_mute_kills_phase(): revert_last_A
  if symmetry_break(): revert_last_edit

  return updated_state, ŒîH*
```

## 11.10 Defaults & Budgets

* (\beta\in[0.01,0.05]) (start 0.02); decay if oscillatory.
* At most one Space edit per window; re-audit after each edit.
* Anneal triggers: (T) improves while (\bar{\zeta}) rises two consecutive windows or E2 tolerance shrinks.

## 11.11 Failure Modes (kill-switches)

* **Amplitude-first error:** amplitude reweighting when (|e_\phi|>\phi_{\text{tol}}) or (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}=0).
* **Brittle gain:** (T\uparrow) with (\bar{\zeta}\uparrow) and no E3 lift‚Äîrevert and anneal.
* **Gauge drift:** (H^*) depends on undeclared gauges‚Äîvoid edits.
* **Over-bridging:** added edges raise ((p+q)) or inflate (\zeta)‚Äîprune and penalize.

## 11.12 Reporting (Œî-Report block)

* (H^*) pre/post per window; component breakdown ((T,\bar{\zeta},\overline{\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}},\overline{(p+q)})).
* MWU telemetry: (\beta) schedule, (|\Delta A|_1), amplitude-lock times.
* Space edits: bridges/prunes with MDL deltas and audit outcomes.
* Mute-test results; symmetry checks; anneal cycles.
* Net (+ŒîH^*) with confidence intervals (bootstrap).

# 12. RG: Why Low-Order Persists (Integer-Thinning & Coarse-Grain)

## 12.1 Purpose

Explain and test the mechanism by which small ((p!+!q)) locks persist while higher-order relations decay under coarse-graining. Provide measurable predictions and procedures.

## 12.2 Flow Equation & Scaling Fields

Lock strength (K_{p:q}) evolves with scale (\ell) by
[
\frac{dK_{p:q}}{d\ell}=(2-\Delta_{p:q})K_{p:q}-\Lambda K_{p:q}^3,\qquad
\Delta_{p:q}=d+\eta(p+q)+\zeta,\Big|\tfrac{\omega_b}{\omega_a}-\tfrac{q}{p}\Big|.
]

* (d): effective dimension (domain-specific).
* (\eta>0): MDL penalty on integer order.
* (\zeta\ge0): brittleness/detune penalty.
* (\Lambda>0): nonlinear saturation.

**Persistence condition.** (2-\Delta_{p:q}>0) at admissible detune ‚áí (K_{p:q}) grows or remains finite; else (K_{p:q}\to0).

## 12.3 Integer-Thinning (Operational Signature)

After detune normalization,
[
\log \hat K_{p:q} ;\approx; \alpha - \beta,(p+q) \quad(\beta>0),
]
with (\beta) weakly increasing under pooling. Surviving ratios form a prefix of the low-order roster (R_L).

## 12.4 Coarse-Graining Operators (√ó2)

## 12.5 Predictions (falsifiable)

## 12.6 Estimation & Normalization

## 12.7 Protocol (RG Audit Block)

1. **Baseline.** Compute (\tilde K_{p:q}), (\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}), (\varepsilon_{\text{stab}}), (\zeta); fit thinning line.
2. **Pool √ó2.** Apply operator; recompute metrics; refit thinning line.
3. **Compare.** Record survivor set, (\Delta\beta), (\Delta\alpha), changes in (\varepsilon,\zeta).
4. **Optionally iterate.** Repeat pooling for 2‚Äì3 steps; track monotonic trends.
5. **Nudge spot-check.** On one survivor and one high-order candidate: run E3 micro-nudges pre/post pooling; compare lifts.

## 12.8 Pseudocode

```

```

# 14. Observer Low-Pass (Œî-Uncertainty: Œîœâ¬∑Œît ‚â≥ c)

## 14.1 Purpose

Constrain claims by the observer‚Äôs finite bandwidth and latency. Enforce a **low-pass discipline** so estimates remain gauge-stable, nudges are safe, and audits are valid.

## 14.2 Constraint

**Resolution bound.** For any window (w) of duration (\Delta t) and effective mainlobe width (\Delta\omega),
[
\Delta\omega\cdot\Delta t ;\gtrsim; c,\qquad c\in[1,,2\pi] \text{ (declare and fix)}.
]

* If the claimed detune (|p f_a-q f_b|) < ( \Delta\omega/(2\pi)), frequency decisions are under-resolved.
* If expected nudge effects occur faster than (\Delta t), causal attribution (E3) is invalid.

## 14.3 Observer Budget Fields (per window)

* **Window spec.** (\Delta t), taper, overlap, latency.
* **Spectral optics.** FFT size, band, mainlobe width (\Delta\omega).
* **Noise floor.** Phase/amp variance; effective damping proxy (\Gamma_{\text{obs}}).
* **Action budget.** Max snaps/nudges; admissible (\delta f/f), (\delta\phi); safety corridor ([K_{\min},K_{\max}]).

## 14.4 Eligibility Under Observation

A candidate ((a,b,p{:}q)) is observer-eligible only if:
[
\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}>0,\quad |s_f|=\varepsilon_{\text{cap}}|p f_a-q f_b|\le\tau_f,\quad
|p f_a-q f_b|\ge \rho_f,\frac{\Delta\omega}{2\pi},
]
with (\rho_f\in[1,2]) (resolution margin). Otherwise, frequency edits defer; widen windows or re-band.

## 14.5 Nudge Timing & Lag

* **Causal minimum lag.** Record pre/post metrics over (\ge L_{\min}) frames with (L_{\min}=\lceil \tau_{\text{sys}}/\Delta t\rceil), where (\tau_{\text{sys}}) is the slowest mode‚Äôs response time (declare).
* **Lag model.** Fit a first-order response (y_{t+1}= (1-\lambda) y_t + \lambda y^\star); report (\hat\lambda) and CI.
* **Rejection.** If measured lag < 1 frame or > budgeted horizon, mark E3 inconclusive.

## 14.6 Windowing Policy

* **Stationarity gate.** Require stationarity tests to pass over each window (drift and variance bounds).
* **Overlap rule.** Use ‚â•50% overlap to stabilize phase continuity if (\Delta\omega) is tight.
* **Taper choice.** Declare and fix (e.g., Hann); do not switch taper within a study without re-running E0.

## 14.7 Gauge Hygiene

* **Phase gauge.** Fix reference channel or use mean-phase anchor (\arg X); log all re-anchoring events.
* **Permutation invariance.** Reindexing must not alter (S^*) or leader; test per window.
* **Time reversal.** Run reversal once per segment; verify directed terms flip sign only.

## 14.8 Safe Action Bounds

* **Frequency retune.** (|\delta f/f|\le2%) and (|\delta f|\le \Delta\omega/(4\pi)).
* **Phase snap.** (5^\circ)‚Äì(15^\circ) once per axis per window.
* **Anneal heat.** Random micro-phase ‚â§ (5^\circ) RMS.
* **Space edits.** ‚â§1 per window; require E2 check immediately after.

## 14.9 Under-Resolution Handling

If (|p f_a-q f_b| < \Delta\omega/(2\pi)):

* **Defer Phase.** No phase trims; increase (\Delta t) or re-band to shrink (\Delta\omega).
* **Stack evidence.** Aggregate two windows (pooled √ó2) and re-evaluate eligibility.
* **Report.** Flag ‚Äúunder-resolved‚Äù in Œî-Report; withhold promotion.

## 14.10 Over-Drive Handling

If a nudge would exceed ([K_{\min},K_{\max}]) or induce slips:

* **Clip.** Scale nudge to corridor; re-run eligibility first.
* **Abort.** If clipping still violates bounds, cancel the nudge and mark E3 ‚Äúnot attempted (safety).‚Äù

## 14.11 Noise & Damping Coupling

Tie observer noise to effective damping:
[
\Gamma_{\text{obs}} \equiv \frac{\sigma_\theta^2}{\Delta t},\qquad
\zeta \uparrow \text{ as } \Gamma_{\text{obs}} \uparrow.
]

* **Mitigation.** Average over overlaps; denoise phases (in-gauge); never denoise labels used in audits without E0 redo.

## 14.12 Audit Hooks

* **E0:** Publish (\Delta\omega\Delta t) products, stationarity curves, null CI checks.
* **E1:** Verify phase evidence persists under amplitude mute at current optics.
* **E2:** Show invariance under declared gauges/permutations with these optics.
* **E3:** Confirm lag-consistent lifts; reject sub-frame ‚Äúinstant‚Äù lifts.
* **E4:** Pool √ó2 using the same optics class; re-state (\Delta\omega'\Delta t' \gtrsim c).

# 18. Kirchhoff/Impedance & Flows; Leadership Adjudication

## 18.1 Purpose

Quantify **who leads** and **where energy/authority flows** using network analogs: potentials, currents, impedances, and conserved balances. Provide operational hooks for promotion, pruning, and safe handovers.

## 18.2 Graph Model

Modes are nodes (i\in V); candidate locks are edges (e=(i!\leftrightarrow!j,p{:}q)\in E). Each edge carries:

* **Coupling (pull)** (K_e\ge0) (rad/s), **damping** (\Gamma_e=\Gamma_i+\Gamma_j).
* **Capture** (\varepsilon_e=[2\pi K_e-\Gamma_e]*+), **stability** (\varepsilon*{\text{stab},e}), **brittleness** (\zeta_e).
* **Phase error** (e_\phi^{(e)}=\mathrm{wrap}(p\theta_j-q\theta_i)), signal (s_\phi^{(e)}=\sin e_\phi^{(e)}).

Define node **potential** (V_i=\theta_i-\arg X) (gauge-invariant reference) and **current** on edge (e=i!\to!j):
[
I_e=\kappa_e,\sin!\big(pV_j-qV_i\big),\qquad \kappa_e=\frac{K_e}{Z_e},
]
with **impedance**
[

]

## 18.3 Kirchhoff Laws (Œî analogs)

* **KCL (node balance).** Net current into each node is bounded by observer and corridor:
  [
  \sum_{e\in \partial i} I_e = \eta_i,\quad |\eta_i|\le \eta_{\max} \ (\text{model mismatch/leak}).
  ]
* **KVL (cycle consistency).** Sum of phase drops around any cycle (C) is a winding:
  [
  \sum_{e\in C} \Delta\phi_e = 2\pi m,\quad m\in\mathbb{Z}.
  ]
  For (S^3)-like vacua, (m=0) on all fundamental cycles.

## 18.4 Power & Torque Diagnostics

Define **power** transferred on (e=i!\to!j):
[

]
**Node power** (P_i=\sum_{e\in\partial i:,e=i\to *} P_e - \sum_{e\in\partial i:,e=*\to i} P_e).
**Torque** proxy (tendency to rotate phases):
[
\tau_i = \sum_{e\in\partial i} \kappa_e,\sin!\big(pV_j-qV_i\big).
]
Leaders exhibit **positive inbound power** (they attract) with **low impedance** paths to followers.

## 18.5 Leadership Score (operational)

For candidate (i), compute multiplicative score
[

where:

* (E_i\in[0,1]): audit pass vector compressed to scalar.
* (Q_i'=Q_i/\max_j Q_j).
* (K_i=\sum_{e\in\partial i}\varpi_e K_e) (MDL-weights (\varpi_e\propto1/(p+q))).
* (P_i^+=\max(P_i,0)) normalized; (P_i=P_i^+) used.
* (C_i) node contribution (mean of positive (C_i) clips).
* (\alpha_i) MDL advantage (penalize high order).
* (R_i=\big(1+\bar{\zeta}_i\big)^{-1}) robustness.
* (W=\sum w_\bullet) (declare weights).

**Handover rule (audited).** Switch leader (\ell\to j) iff:

1. (L_j \ge (1+\rho),L_\ell) with (\rho\ge0.05);
2. ((p+q)*j \le (p+q)*\ell) (MDL non-worse);

4. If at or above L3: E3 shows (\Delta K,\Delta\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},\Delta T>0) for (j) on held-out nudges.

## 18.6 Flow-Based Pruning & Bridging

* **Prune:** Edges with **high impedance** (Z_e) and **negative marginal power** ((P_e\le0)) across (W) windows; edges that raise (\bar{\zeta}) or flatten thinning slope.
* **Bridge:** Add edge (b) that **reduces total path impedance** between clusters and **increases inbound power** to current leader or its challenger while lowering ((p+q)).

**Impedance path metric.**
[
\mathrm{Zpath}(u\leadsto v)=\min_{\pi:u\to v}\sum_{e\in \pi} Z_e,\quad \text{(use Dijkstra with weights }Z_e).
]
Accept a bridge (b) if it lowers (\mathrm{Zpath}) by ‚â• threshold and passes E1‚ÄìE2, with E3 lift on (K,\varepsilon_{\text{cap}},T).

## 18.7 Stability of Leadership (hysteresis)

* Require advantage (L_j/L_\ell\ge 1+\rho) for (W) consecutive windows (default (W=3)).
* Record **pre/post** (P_i, Z_e, \varepsilon) maps; roll back if E2 symmetry or E3 causality fails post-switch.

## 27.11 Failure modes (kill-switches)

* **Supercritical chain:** A triad ladder with (\chi_n>1) that survives E3 (causal lift) **and** E4 (pooling) ‚Üí **fail** smoothness under Œî-test optics.
* **Amplitude illusion:** Triad coherence vanishes under amplitude mute (E1 fail).
* **Symmetry drift:** (\chi,\ S^*,) or survivor set changes under rotations/translations/helical reparam (E2 fail).
* **RG inversion:** High-order/remote triads outrun local low-order after pooling (E4 fail).
* **Eligibility breach:** Phase actions with (|s_f^{(n)}|>1) or (\varepsilon_{\text{cap},n}=0).
  Actions: revert edits; tighten shells and gauges; prune brittle/high-order triads; re-audit.
